About Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand, often called Devbhumi (Land of Gods), is a jewel of northern India. Nestled in the Himalayas, it is home to sacred rivers, snow-clad peaks, lush valleys, and vibrant culture. From the Char Dham pilgrimage to thrilling treks, wildlife safaris, yoga retreats, and serene hill stations, Uttarakhand offers a complete travel experience.

Char Dham Yatra in Uttarakhand

The Char Dham Yatra is one of the most sacred pilgrimages in India. It covers four holy shrines located in the Himalayas ‚Äî Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri. Pilgrims believe that visiting these shrines washes away sins and opens the path to salvation.

Kedarnath

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, located near Mandakini River at 3,583m altitude.

Badrinath

Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, nestled between Nar and Narayan mountain ranges.

Gangotri

Source of River Ganga, dedicated to Goddess Ganga, located at 3,100m altitude.

Yamunotri

Origin of River Yamuna, dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, surrounded by snow peaks.
